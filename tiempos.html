--PRESENT--

PRESENT SIMPLE

Se usa para hablar de hábitos o rutinas, hechos o verdades generales, situaciones permanentes, horarios fijos o eventos programados.

Estructura:

Afirmativa: Sujeto + Verbo (s/es tercera persona) + Complemento.

She He works every day.

I You They We eat banana every morning.

Negativa: Sujeto + Don't (I, We, They, You) Doesn't (She, He, It) + Verbo + Complemento.

She He doesn't play basketboll every week.

I You They We don´t drink too much coffee.

Interrogativa: Do (We, They, You) Does (She, He, It) + Sujeto + Verbo + Complemento?

Do You They We speak English?

Does She He live here?

Nota:
  • Se agrega una "S" o "ES" cuando sea tercera persona (He, She, It).

      • play -- plays

      go -- goes

      study -- studies

PRESENT CONTINUOUS

Se usa para hablar de acciones que están ocurriendo ahora, acciones temporales (no permanentes), planes futuros ya decididos y cambios que están ocurriendo.

Estructura:

Afirmativa: Sujeto + Am (I) Is (She, He, It) Are (You, They, We) + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

I am studying now.

She He is singing.

You They We are working.

Negativa: Sujeto + Am not (I) Isn´t (She, He, It) Aren't (You, They, We) + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

I am not studying English right now.

She He isn´t watching TV.

You They We aren´t eating cookies.

Interrogativa: Am (I) Is (She, He, It) Are (We, They, You) + Sujeto + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

Am I working?

Is She He reading the newspaper in the living room?

Are You They We eating again?

Nota:
  • Si el verbo termina con "e", se elimina la e.
    make → making
    • Si el verbo termina en vocal + consonante y es corto.
      run → running
      • Si el verbo termina en "ie", se cambia por y.
        lie → lying
        • Hay verbos que no se usan con ing, como:
          like, love, hate, want, know, believe, etc.

PRESENT PERFECT

Se usa para hablar una acción que ocurrió en el pasado, pero sin decir cuando, experiencias de vida, acción pasada con resultado en el presente, acciones que empezaron en el pasado y continúan.

Estructura:

Afirmativa: Sujeto + Have (They, We, You, I) Has (She, He) + Verbo en pasado participio + Complemento.

She He has arrived.

I You They We have eaten fish.

Negativa: Sujeto + Haven´t (I, You, We, They) Hasn´t (She, He, It) + Verbo en pasado participio + Complemento.

She He hasn´t eaten meat.

I You They We haven´t visited USA.

Interrogativa: Have (I, We, They, You) Has (She, He, It) + Sujeto + Verbo en -ing pasado participio + Complemento ?

Has She He seen that movie?

Have I You They We finished?

Nota:
  • Se usan expresiones como:
    • just → justo ahora/recién
    • already → ya
    • yet → aún/ya (negativo o pregunta)
    • since → desde
    • for → por (duración de tiempo)
    • ever → alguna vez
    • never → nunca

    PRESENT PERFECT CONTINUOUS

    Se usa para hablar de acciones que comenzaron en el pasado y siguen ahora, acciones recientes que dejaron evidencia o efecto visible y enfatizar la duración de una actividad.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Have (They, We, You, I) Has (She, He) + Been + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    She He has been reading for two hours.

    I You They We have been studying.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Haven´t (They, We, You, I) Hasn´t (She, He) + Been + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    She He hasn´t been sleeping well.

    I You They We haven´t been living here since January.

    Interrogativa: Have (I, We, They, You) Has (She, He, It) + Sujeto + Been + Verbo en -ing + Complemento ?

    Has She He been working all day?

    Have I You They We been studying English?

    Nota:
  • Se usan expresiones como:
    • since → desde
    • for → por (duración de tiempo)
    • lately/recently → últimamente/recientemente
    • how long → ¿cuánto tiempo?
    --PAST--

    PAST SIMPLE

    Se usa para hablar de acciones que ya ocurrieron en el pasado y terminaron y acciones consecutivas en una historia.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Verbo en pasado + Complemento.

    She ate bagel.

    I visited my grandmother yesterday.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Did not (didn´t) + Verbo en forma base + Complemento.

    They didn´t go to school last week.

    He didn´t drink alcohol last month.

    Interrogativa: Did + Sujeto + Verbo en forma base + Complemento?

    Did you see the movie?

    Did he go to school?

    Nota:
  • Se usan expresiones como:
    • yesterday → ayer
    • last night → anoche
    • last week → la semana pasada
    • in 2020 → en 2020
    • two day ago → hace dos días
    • when I was a child → cuando era niño(a)
  • Los verbos irregulares cambian su forma.
    • go → went
    • eat → ate
  • Los verbos regulares se añade -ed.
    • walk → walked
    • clean → cleaned

    PAST CONTINUOUS

    Se usa para hablar de acciones en progreso en un momento específico del pasado, hablar de dos acciones que ocurren al mismo tiempo en el pasado y acciones en progreso interrumpida por otra.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Was (I, She, He, It) Were (You, They, We) + Verbo en -ing + Complemento.

    I She He was studying all the night.

    You They We were working at 5 yesterday.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Wasn´t (I, She, He, It) Weren´t (You, They, We) + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    I She He wasn´t paying attention.

    You They We weren´t listening music.

    Interrogativa: Was (I, She, He, It) Were (We, They, You) + Sujeto + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    Was I She He sleeping during the storm?

    Were You They We working at 8 yesterday?

    Nota:
  • Se usan estas palabras en ocasiones:
    • while → mientras
    • when → cuando
    • at the moment → en ese momento
    • all day → todo el día
    • all night → toda la noche

    PAST PERFECT

    Se usa para hablar sobre una acción antes que otra en el pasado, para dar contexto o antecedentes en una historia.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Had + Verbo en pasado participio + Complemento.

    She had finished her homework.

    I had studied before the test started.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Had not (hadn´t) + Verbo en pasado participio + Complemento.

    They hadn´t seen the movie.

    We hadn´t eaten chilaquiles.

    Interrogativa: Had + Sujeto + Verbo en pasado participio + Complemento.

    Had you finished the work before leaving?

    Had they eaten before the meeting?

    Nota:
  • Se usan palabras como:
    • before → antes
    • after → después
    • already → ya
    • never → nunca
    • just → justo
    • when → cuando
    • by the time → para cuando

    PAST PERFECT CONTINUOUS

    Se usa para hablar sobre una acción que estaba ocurriendo en el pasado antes de otra acción o momento y para expresar la duración o causa de algo en el pasado.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Had been + Verbo en -ing + Complemento.

    She had been waiting for 30 minutes when the bus arrived.

    I had been studying.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Had not (hadn´t) been Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    He hadn´t been sleeping well.

    It hadn´t been raining all day.

    Interrogativa: Had + Sujeto + Been + Verbo con - ing + Complemento.

    Had they been working all day?

    Had he been studying a lot before the exam?

    Nota:
  • Se usan palabras como:
    • for → durante(período de tiempo)
    • since → desde(un punto específico)
    • when → cuando
    • before → antes
    • how long → ¿cuánto tiempo?
    • by the time → para cuando
    --FUTURE--

    FUTURE SIMPLE

    Se usa para hablar sobre predicciones, decisiones espontáneas(en el momento), promesas, ofrecimientos o amenazas y hechos inevitables o ciertos.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Will + Verbo en forma base + Complemento.

    I will travel next year.

    It will rain tomorrow.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Will not (won´t) + Verbo en forma base + Complemento.

    They won´t eat pizza tonight.

    She won´t come to the party.

    Interrogativa: Will + Sujeto + Verbo en forma base + Complemento?

    Will you come to my party?

    Will you help me?

    Nota:
  • Se usan expresiones como:
    • tomorrow → mañana
    • next week/month/year → la próxima semana/mes/año
    • soon → pronto
    • later → más tarde
    • I think → creo
    • I hope → espero
    • I believe → supongo
    • in 2026 → en 2026

    FUTURE CONTINUOUS

    Se usa para hablar sobre una acción que estará ocurriendo en un momento del futuro, acción futura en progreso interrumpida por otra o una acción planeada en el futuro.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Will be + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    I will be studying.

    This time tomorrow, I will be flying to New York.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Won´t be + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    They won´t be watching TV at that time.

    She won´t be working tomorrow.

    Interrogativa: Will + Sujeto + Be verbo con -ing + Complemento?

    Will you be traveling next week?

    Will you be using the computer later?

    Nota:
  • Se usan expresiones como:
    • at this time tomorrow → a esta hora mañana
    • next week/year → la próxima semana/año
    • in two hours → en dos horas
    • when → cuando
    • while → mientras

    FUTURE PERFECT

    Se usa para hablar sobre una acción que se habrá completado antes de un punto específico en el futuro y para hacer suposiciones o predicciones sobre el pasado desde el futuro.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Will have + Verbo en pasado participio + Complemento.

    I will have finished my work by 8:00 pm.

    By the next week, I will have cleaned the entire house.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Won´t have + Verbo en pasado participio + Complemento.

    They won´t have arrived before the meeting starts.

    She won´t have arrived by 10.

    Interrogativa: Will + Sujeto Have verbo en pasado participio + Complemento?

    Will you have completed the project?

    Will he have finished the report by tomorrow?

    Nota:
  • Se usan expresiones como:
    • by (tomorrow, next year, 5 pm, etc) → pára(mañana,el próximo año, las 5 pm,etc)
    • before → antes
    • by the time → para cuando
    • already → ya(en el contexto futuro)

    FUTURE PERFECT CONTINUOUS

    Se usa para hablar de la duración de una acción que habrá estado ocurriendo hasta cierto punto en el futuro o enfatizar cuánto tiempo habrá estado ocurriendo una acción continua.

    Estructura:

    Afirmativa: Sujeto + Will have been + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    I will have been working for 5 hours.

    Next month, they will have been living here for a year.

    Negativa: Sujeto + Won´t have been + Verbo con -ing + Complemento.

    They won´t have been practing much.

    She won´t have been studying all day.

    Interrogativa: Will + Sujeto Have been + Verbo con -ing + Complemento?

    Will she have been waiting long?

    How long will you have been studying for that exam?

    Nota:
  • Se usan expresiones como:
    • by (a time/date) → para(una hora/fecha)
    • for → durante
    • when → cuando
    • how long → ¿cuánto tiempo?
